# E8 Mass Spectrum

## Overview

When the 2D Ising model at criticality is perturbed by a
**longitudinal magnetic field** $h$ (breaking $\mathbb{Z}_2$
explicitly while remaining at $T = T_c$), the resulting field theory
is integrable and possesses a spectrum of **8 stable particles**
whose mass ratios are determined by the $E_8$ exceptional Lie algebra.

This is one of the most remarkable connections between condensed
matter physics and pure mathematics: the root system of $E_8$
(rank 8, dimension 248) governs the exact mass spectrum of a
magnetic perturbation of a $c = 1/2$ CFT.

---

## Mass Ratios

| Particle | $m_n / m_1$ | Approximate value |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| $m_1$ | $1$ | $1$ |
| $m_2$ | $2\cos(\pi/5)$ | $1.6180\ldots = \varphi$ (golden ratio) |
| $m_3$ | $2\cos(\pi/30)$ | $1.9890\ldots$ |
| $m_4$ | $4\cos(\pi/5)\cos(7\pi/30)$ | $2.4049\ldots$ |
| $m_5$ | $4\cos(\pi/5)\cos(2\pi/15)$ | $2.9563\ldots$ |
| $m_6$ | $4\cos(\pi/5)\cos(\pi/30)$ | $3.2183\ldots$ |
| $m_7$ | $8\cos^2(\pi/5)\cos(7\pi/30)$ | $3.8912\ldots$ |
| $m_8$ | $8\cos^2(\pi/5)\cos(2\pi/15)$ | $4.7833\ldots$ |

The most striking feature is $m_2/m_1 = \varphi = (1 + \sqrt{5})/2$,
the golden ratio. Only particles $m_1$--$m_3$ are below the
two-particle threshold $2m_1$; particles $m_4$--$m_8$ are stable
due to integrability constraints that forbid their decay.

---

## Experimental Confirmation

Coldea et al. (2010) observed the $E_8$ mass spectrum in the
quasi-1D Ising ferromagnet CoNb$_2$O$_6$ (cobalt niobate). In a
transverse magnetic field near the quantum critical point, neutron
scattering revealed two bound states with mass ratio

$$m_2 / m_1 = 1.618(3) \approx \varphi$$

in agreement with Zamolodchikov's prediction. This constituted the
first direct experimental observation of $E_8$ symmetry in a
condensed matter system.
